# ITK: stakeholder Map & Matrix

Help a product practitioner apply **stakeholder Map & Matrix** from the MITRE Innovation Toolkit.

**Phase:** SCOPE · **Difficulty:** Intermediate · **Group:** 4+ people · **Time:** 45+ minutes

> Look across stakeholders and categorize them according to key variables (e.g., interest, influence, impact).

---

## When This Tool Fits Best

- Early discovery when launching a cross-functional initiative with many stakeholders whose influence and interest are unclear
- Preparing an engagement plan before a roadmap review where executives hold conflicting priorities
- Onboarding to a new product area and needing to rapidly map the political landscape and decision-makers
- Planning change management for a platform migration that affects multiple internal teams differently
- Sequencing stakeholder buy-in before an OKR commitment that requires resources from skeptical partner teams

---

## Key Concepts

**Power-Interest Grid** — A 2x2 matrix plotting stakeholders by their power to affect the project and their interest in its outcome. It produces four engagement strategies: manage closely, keep satisfied, keep informed, and monitor.

**Paired Variables** — The two dimensions chosen for each axis (e.g., influence vs. interest, or cost-to-maintain vs. strategic value). Running multiple paired analyses reveals relationships a single grid would hide.

**Quadrant Migration** — The deliberate strategy of moving a stakeholder from their current quadrant to a desired one through targeted actions. This turns a static snapshot into an actionable engagement roadmap.

**Influence vs. Impact** — Distinguishing stakeholders who shape decisions (influence) from those who bear the consequences (impact). High-impact but low-influence groups are often overlooked yet critical for adoption.

**Representative Diversity** — Ensuring the stakeholder set spans the full range of affected roles, functions, and perspectives — not just the loudest or most accessible voices. Gaps here create blind spots in scope and adoption risk.

**Engagement Strategy** — The differentiated communication and involvement approach assigned per quadrant, ranging from deep collaboration to light monitoring. It allocates limited PM bandwidth where it yields the most leverage.

---

## Common Pitfalls to Watch For

- Treating the map as a one-time artifact instead of revisiting it as influence and interest shift across project phases, leaving the engagement plan stale and misaligned.
- Choosing vague or redundant axis variables (e.g., 'importance' vs. 'priority') so quadrants blur together and produce no actionable distinction.
- Plotting only the obvious, accessible stakeholders and omitting silent high-impact groups, which surfaces as adoption resistance after launch.
- Stopping at categorization without defining specific migration actions and owners, so the matrix becomes a wall decoration rather than an engagement plan.
- Conflating influence with seniority — assuming the org chart equals real decision power — and under-engaging the people who actually sway outcomes.
- Doing the mapping solo rather than with the project team, baking in one person's political assumptions and missing relationship intel others hold.

---

## How to Help

- **Read context first.** Before asking anything, check what the user has already shared — their role, product, team size, timeline, what phase of work they're in.
- **Experienced users can skip straight to the work.** If they name this tool directly or describe a clear situation, help them run it — don't force a guided flow.
- **Use the Adaptive Decision Ladder when the situation is unclear.** Offer numbered options; the user can reply `2`, `1 & 3`, or describe freely. Skip steps that context already resolves.
- **Walk through the facilitation steps** from the full skill reference, adapting language and examples to their specific product context.
- **Surface pitfalls proactively** — especially those most likely given what they've shared.
- **Suggest complementary tools** when the conversation reveals adjacent needs.
- **Stay grounded in product artifacts**: PRDs, OKRs, roadmaps, user stories, sprint ceremonies, stakeholder briefings.
