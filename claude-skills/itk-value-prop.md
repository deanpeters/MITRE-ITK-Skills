# ITK: Value Proposition Canvas

Help a product practitioner apply **Value Proposition Canvas** from the MITRE Innovation Toolkit.

**Phase:** UNDERSTAND · **Difficulty:** Intermediate · **Group:** 4+ people · **Time:** 45+ minutes

> Map user profiles (i.e., jobs, pains, and gains) to values (i.e., gain creators, products and services, pain relievers) to ensure that a product meets user needs.

---

## When This Tool Fits Best

- Validating that a proposed feature set actually addresses prioritized customer jobs before committing engineering capacity in a PRD
- Repositioning a struggling product by exposing gaps between marketed benefits and the pains users actually feel
- Early discovery sprints where the team needs to ground solution ideas in evidenced customer jobs, pains, and gains
- Aligning sales, marketing, and product on a single articulation of differentiated value before a launch
- Pressure-testing a new offering's assumptions before building an MVP or running a Lean Startup experiment

---

## Key Concepts

**Customer Profile** — The right-side circle capturing customer jobs, pains, and gains derived from research rather than assumption. It defines the demand side that any value proposition must earn fit against.

**Value Map** — The left-side square listing products and services, pain relievers, and gain creators your offering provides. It represents the supply side the team controls and designs.

**Customer Jobs** — The functional, social, and emotional tasks customers are trying to accomplish, closely related to Jobs-to-be-Done. Naming jobs precisely prevents teams from solving for tasks no one actually performs.

**Pains and Gains** — Pains are the negative outcomes, risks, and obstacles customers experience; gains are the benefits and outcomes they desire. Ranking them by severity and importance separates must-fix issues from nice-to-haves.

**Problem-Solution Fit** — The state where your pain relievers and gain creators map directly to the customer's most important pains and gains. Fit on the canvas is a hypothesis to validate, not proof of market demand.

**Pain Relievers vs. Gain Creators** — Pain relievers eliminate or reduce things customers dread; gain creators produce outcomes customers want. Distinguishing them keeps the team from conflating risk reduction with upside generation.

---

## Common Pitfalls to Watch For

- Populating the Customer Profile from internal opinion or sales anecdotes instead of real user research, which produces a value map that confirms existing biases rather than customer reality.
- Listing every job, pain, and gain without ranking them by severity and importance, so the team optimizes for trivial needs while the deal-breaker pains go unaddressed.
- Building the Value Map first and reverse-engineering customer needs to justify it, which inverts the tool and guarantees false fit.
- Confusing features (products and services) with the actual pain relievers and gain creators they deliver, leaving the team unable to explain why anyone should care.
- Treating a clean on-canvas fit as validated demand and skipping experimentation, then discovering at launch that customers won't pay or switch.
- Creating a single generic canvas for multiple distinct personas, blurring conflicting jobs and pains into an offering that serves no one well.

---

## How to Help

- **Read context first.** Before asking anything, check what the user has already shared — their role, product, team size, timeline, what phase of work they're in.
- **Experienced users can skip straight to the work.** If they name this tool directly or describe a clear situation, help them run it — don't force a guided flow.
- **Use the Adaptive Decision Ladder when the situation is unclear.** Offer numbered options; the user can reply `2`, `1 & 3`, or describe freely. Skip steps that context already resolves.
- **Walk through the facilitation steps** from the full skill reference, adapting language and examples to their specific product context.
- **Surface pitfalls proactively** — especially those most likely given what they've shared.
- **Suggest complementary tools** when the conversation reveals adjacent needs.
- **Stay grounded in product artifacts**: PRDs, OKRs, roadmaps, user stories, sprint ceremonies, stakeholder briefings.
