# ITK: Culture Building Canvas

Help a product practitioner apply **Culture Building Canvas** from the MITRE Innovation Toolkit.

**Phase:** SCOPE · **Difficulty:** Intermediate · **Group:** 4+ people · **Time:** 60+ minutes

> A framework based on “influence channels” that shape and define an organization’s culture.

---

## When This Tool Fits Best

- Scaling a startup where founder-driven norms break down and culture must be made explicit across new teams
- Post-merger or reorg integration when two product orgs hold conflicting norms about risk, speed, and ownership
- Shifting an engineering org from output-focused delivery to outcome-driven discovery and experimentation
- Rebuilding psychological safety and transparency after layoffs, attrition, or a high-profile product failure
- Establishing shared cultural attributes when launching a new innovation lab or cross-functional product group

---

## Key Concepts

**Influence Channels** — The mechanisms through which culture is actually transmitted — symbols, behaviors, systems, and rituals. Culture change fails when leaders broadcast values verbally but leave these channels unchanged.

**Four Quadrants** — The canvas divides influence into four categories (typically symbols, systems, behaviors, and stories/rituals) so teams design coherent change across all of them rather than relying on a single lever like a mission statement.

**Message Coherence** — The principle that enthusiasm in one channel can be silently contradicted by inaction in another. Culture forms from the consistency of signals, not their volume, so contradictions must be hunted and resolved.

**Action Sentences** — Specific, attributable commitments phrased as 'Person X does Y with Z' rather than abstract aspirations. They convert vague culture goals into observable, accountable behaviors teams can verify.

**Desired Culture Exemplars** — Existing people, teams, or organizations that already embody the target attributes. Borrowing proven patterns shortens change cycles and gives the team concrete models instead of inventing norms from scratch.

**Espoused vs. Enacted Values** — The gap between the values an org claims and the behaviors it rewards. The canvas surfaces this gap by forcing teams to map actual channel signals against stated cultural aspirations.

---

## Common Pitfalls to Watch For

- Filling only the 'symbols and stories' quadrants while ignoring systems and incentives, producing slogans and posters that change nothing measurable.
- Writing vague aspirations like 'be more collaborative' instead of attributable action sentences, leaving no one accountable and no way to verify progress.
- Skipping the contradiction-hunting step, so leadership's verbal enthusiasm gets silently undercut by unchanged promotion criteria or budget allocations.
- Treating the canvas as a one-time offsite artifact rather than revisiting it as behaviors shift, so the plan goes stale and credibility erodes.
- Assembling a culture team of only managers, missing the channels ICs actually experience daily and producing a top-down plan that doesn't land.
- Expecting fast, visible results and abandoning the plan when metrics don't move in a sprint, despite culture change requiring sustained multi-quarter reinforcement.

## How to Help

- **Read context first.** Before asking anything, check what the user has already shared — their role, product, team size, timeline, what phase of work they're in.
- **Experienced users can skip straight to the work.** If they name this tool directly or describe a clear situation, help them run it — don't force a guided flow.
- **Use the Adaptive Decision Ladder when the situation is unclear.** Offer numbered options; the user can reply `2`, `1 & 3`, or describe freely. Skip steps that context already resolves.
- **Walk through the facilitation steps** from the full skill reference, adapting language and examples to their specific product context.
- **Surface pitfalls proactively** — especially those most likely given what they've shared.
- **Suggest complementary tools** when the conversation reveals adjacent needs.
- **Stay grounded in product artifacts**: PRDs, OKRs, roadmaps, user stories, sprint ceremonies, stakeholder briefings.
